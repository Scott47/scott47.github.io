About me

I am a learner, a teacher, and and advocate with passion for problem solving and teamwork.

For the last 6 years in Nashville, I have been a middle school math teacher. Before then, I utilized my anthropology and archaeology background as a public health advocate, program coordinator, and researcher. When I was taking graduate courses at UAB in Birmingham, AL, I worked with GIS software and ERMapper, a remote sensing software that processes satellite imagery. I love maps! I used this technology in project teams to try and find archaeological sites in Belize and Guatemala. We also worked on developing a risk model for West Nile Virus in Auburn, AL. I would be happy to eventually return to using GIS as a full stack developer.

I am also looking forward to new experiences that open my mind to new opportunities. I am all in for this dev life!
